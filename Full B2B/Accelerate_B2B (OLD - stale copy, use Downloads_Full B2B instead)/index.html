Frequently asked questions

  • No catch, but there is a filter. A test campaign costs us real time and money, so we only run them for businesses we expect to win with: a proven offer, a customer worth $10,000 or more, and a target market of 10,000+ people. That’s also why spots are limited to a handful each month. If the test works, we talk about continuing. If it doesn’t, you walk away with every reply and lead we generated, free.

  • Your campaign is live within 14 days of onboarding. The first few days go on your ICP session and list building, then the copy gets written while your sending infrastructure warms up. Almost all of that work happens on our side. You’ll know the send date before we start, and replies usually land within days of launch.

  • We won’t promise a number before we’ve seen your market, because honest answers beat big ones. Results depend on your offer, your market size, and how fast your team picks up the replies. What we can show you: real campaigns have produced 95 interested replies in 30 days for one client, and 426 sales opportunities over six months for another. The free test exists so the number you trust is your own.

  • Spam is irrelevance, and irrelevance is what the system filters out. People don’t complain when a recruiter calls about a great role, or an investor asks about their business, because the message fits them. Your campaigns work the same way: every company is qualified against your best clients first, and every message is written for the person reading it. One campaign held a 9.5% reply rate across 261,000 emails. People answer mail that’s about them.

  • Ads bring whoever clicks. Outbound puts you in front of the exact companies you’d choose, because the list is built and qualified before the first email sends. That’s why outbound meetings tend to fit: everyone contacted already matched your best clients. If ads are working, keep them. Outbound runs alongside, and it’s how you land the named accounts your ads never seem to reach.

  • We set up separate sending domains and inboxes for your campaigns, then warm them before any outreach goes out. Your main domain never sends a single cold email, so it never takes the reputation hit. Everything is registered for you, replies land in your inbox or CRM, and if you ever leave, the infrastructure is yours to take.

  • An SDR runs $65K+ a year, takes about 90 days to ramp, and reaches prospects one at a time. The system reaches thousands of qualified companies a month and hands the warm replies to whoever closes your deals. If you have reps, their calendars stay full instead of idle. If you don’t, you only ever talk to interested buyers. Either way, nobody on your payroll spends their day prospecting.

  • No contract and no minimum. After the free test it’s month to month, with no setup fee, and you can stop whenever you like. We keep it that way on purpose: if the results stop, you shouldn’t need a lawyer to leave. It also means we re-earn every client, every month, which is exactly the pressure a results business should be under.

  • Anywhere your buyers read email, outbound can work, so we’re not tied to one industry. So far that includes buy-side M&A, fundraising, and marketing agencies, and the same system carried all three. What matters more than industry is whether your market is big enough to mail for years. The application checks exactly that, so you’ll know quickly either way.
